As the magnificent wild canine known for its intelligence, social nature, and striking physical appearance, the wolf wins my respect. In addition to their features, they look as cute as dogs.
This wolf granny squarefeatures the wolf's signature pointed 3D ears that stand up from the square. We also use color changes to reflect the natural variation in the wolf's fur color.
The muzzle part of the wolf is crocheted after we finish the granny square. It is an open ball that presents a lovely 3D effect. Want more ideas for cute animal granny squares? Check our animal granny square list.
Since this design looks very similar to the dog, you can make both a dog granny square and a dog granny square with single crochet, a perfect set for pet lovers.
Pair it with the cat claws square, as wolf and cat paws have similar shapes. Then add a fox granny square to complete your wild and cozy theme, thanks to their shared pointy ears and clever expressions.

Abbreviations (US Term)
ch- chain stitch
sc- single crochet
hdc- half double crochet
dc- double crochet
slst- slip stitch for joining to a different round
st- stitch
yo- yarn over
sk- skip
inc- increase
pic- picot stitch
TLO- third loop only
Step-by-Step
How to change the color
When you see a color change in the tutorial, work the last stitch of the old color until the final yarn over. Yarn over with the new color and pull through all remaining loops to complete the stitch and color change.
To secure the ends, lay the yarn tails over the stitches of the previous row and work over them as you continue with the new color to weave them in.
Wolf Head
Crochet five rounds of hdc and sc, alternating white and grey yarn to shape the face and head.
Round 1-Foundation loop
With grey yarn: Magic loop, ch1, 7sc, slst to the first sc.
Round 2-Shaping the face
Ch1, sc inc in the same st, sc inc, change to white yarn: (sc inc)x5. Change back to grey yarn on the final yo of the last sc, and slst to the first sc.
Round 3-Expanding the head
For the following 3 rounds, maintain color alignment by working white stitches in white and grey stitches in grey, matching the colors of the previous round.
Ch2 (count as 1hdc), 1hdc in the same st to complete the first hdc inc. Hdc inc, 1hdc; Change to white yarn: Hdc inc, 1hdc, hdc inc, (1sc, sc inc) repeat 2 times, hdc inc, 1hdc, hdc inc; Change back to grey yarn: 1hdc. Slst to the top of the first ch2.
Round 4-Finish the face base
Ch2 (count as 1hdc), 1hdc in the same st to complete the first hdc inc, (hdc inc)x3; Change to white yarn: 1hdc, 1hdc+1sc, 1sc, sc inc, 9sc, sc inc, 1sc, 1sc+1hdc, 1hdc; Change back to grey yarn: hdc inc. Slst to the top of the first ch2.
Round 5-Complete the wolf head
Ch2 (count as 1hdc), 3hdc, (hdc inc)x3; Change to white yarn: 3hdc, 2sc, (sc inc, 4sc) repeat 2 times, sc inc, 2sc, 3hdc; Change back to grey yarn: (hdc inc)x3. Slst to the top of the first ch2, and fasten off.
Squaring
Third loop only. With grey stitches on top, join new yarn to the 4th grey stitch from the right. Ch3 (count as 1dc), 1dc in the same st to complete the first dc inc, 8hdc, dc inc, ch3; [dc inc, 8hdc, dc inc, ch3] repeat 3 times, omit the final ch3. Instead, work ch1, then 1hdc into the top of the first ch3.
Border
Ch3 (count as 1dc), 1dc in the same corner, 1dc in each st around, (2dc, ch3, 2dc) in each corner as you go. When you return to the starting corner, work (2dc, ch3), slst to the top of the first ch3, fasten off.
Wolf's Left Ear
Inner Ear
With grey stitches on top, join white yarn to the 4th grey stitch from the left. Ch2, (1dc, ch-1 pic, 1dc) in the next st, ch2, slst to the next st. Fasten off and weave in the ends.
Ear Edge
Join grey yarn to the st immediately to the right of the inner ear join (the 5th grey st from the left), ch1.
Now we crochet along the inner ear part and work in the TLO: sk the initial ch, 2sc, sc inc, 2sc, ch1. Slst in the same grey st used for the inner ear's final slst. Fasten off, and weave in your ends.
Wolf's Right Ear
Repeat the ear instructions on the opposite side, leaving a 5-stitch for symmetry. (Join white yarn for the inner ear in the 3rd grey stitch from the right.)
Wolf's Muzzle
Create a tiny bowl shape for the muzzle.
Round 1
Magic loop, ch2(count as 1hdc), 9hdc, slst to the top of the first ch2.
Round 2
Ch1+1sc, 1sc in each st around, slst to the first sc. Fasten off, leave a long tail for attaching.
Final Assembly and Details
Attach the Muzzle: Turn the muzzle over so the wrong side faces out. Position it on the lower center of the head and sew it firmly in place. You can add a small amount of stuffing before closing.
The Nose: Stitch the nose on the muzzle.
Eyes & Blushes: Make two French knots for the eyes. Then, use pink yarn to stitch the blushes just below the eyes for a finished look.
