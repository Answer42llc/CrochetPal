Tiny Christmas Wreath
This little wreath is about 4.5cm / 2 ½” diameter if made with worsted yarn and 3.5mm/E hook, and it can
be completed in 5 minutes! You could use it for dollhouse decoration, as an applique on doll clothes or small
accessories, glue it on gift cards, or even turn it into festive earrings after starching.
Tools and materials:
● Yarn in green (about 2m/2.2yd, shown in Caron Simply Soft
Kelly Green) and red (34-40cm/12-16”
, shown in Red Heart
Soft Really Red)- any weight including thread will work, it
will only influence the finished size
● Hook matching your chosen yarn weight (I suggest
3.5mm/E for worsted yarn)
● Yarn needle, pair of scissors
● Optional: clear nail polish or white glue
Abbreviations
Ch: chain or chains; sl st: slip stitch; st: stitch
The pattern
Rnd 1: with green, make a slip knot and ch 14;
sl st in the first ch you made, to join the piece in a circle;
[ch 3, sl st in the next ch of the circle] 14 times (work your
last sl st into the st that joined the circle) (14 chain-loops
made)
Fasten off and weave in the ends (or keep them for sewing, if the
wreath will become an applique).
Take a 30-40cm/12-16" length of red yarn, thread it into a yarn
needle and weave it through the chain loops around the wreath,
starting and ending at the join. Tie the ends in a bow, then cut
them to the length you like.
Optionally, if your yarn holds knots poorly or tends to fray at the
ends, dot some clear nail polish or white glue at the knot and at
the ends, to secure them.
