PATTERN DESCRIPTION
This pattern guides you through creating a Mochi Bear using a flat construction method. The bear
is worked in two identical flat panels, shaped with increases and decreases to form the head and
body. The panels are then joined together and lightly stuffed for a soft, plush finish.
This is not an amigurumi pattern.
The shaping is achieved entirely through structured rows worked back and forth, making it
approachable for confident beginners.
The finished bear is perfect as:
A keychain or bag charm
A decorative plush
A nursery accent
A handmade gift
PLEASE NOTE:
The pattern is written in two parts: first, a complete simplified pattern, followed by a detailed,
step-by-step instructions.
FINISHED SIZE
Size will vary depending on yarn and tension.
Using the recommended materials, the bear measures approximately 3 inches tall.
SUGGESTED MATERIALS
YarnArt Begonia Cotton Fine (2) yarn
2.0 mm crochet hook
Yarn needle
Scissors
Fiberfill stuffing
ABBREVIATIONS (US TERMS)
fc = foundation chain

sp = space
sc = single crochet
ss = slip stitch
inc = increase (2 sc in same stitch)
dec = decrease (single crochet 2 stitches together)
hdc = half double crochet
dc = double crochet
FO = fasten off

PATTERN INSTRUCTIONS
HEAD
FOUNDATION: CH 4
ROW 1: 3 SC (3)
Insert your hook into the 2nd chain from the hook.
Make 1 single crochet in each chain across.
You will have 3 single crochets total.
👉 Stitch count: 3
ROW 2: INC, 1 SC, INC (5)
Chain 1
Turn your work.
Make 2 single crochets in the first stitch (increase*)
Make 1 single crochet in the next stitch
Make 2 single crochets in the last stitch (increase)
You will have 5 stitches total.
👉 Stitch count: 5
*INCREASE
An increase (inc) means
making two stitches in the
same stitch.
Two single crochet made
into the same stitch
counts as one increase.
This pattern uses the
single crochet increase.
ROW 3: INC, 3 SC, INC (7)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make 2 single crochets in the first stitch
Make 1 single crochet in each of the next 3 stitches
Make 2 single crochets in the last stitch
👉 Stitch count: 7
ROW 4: INC, 5 SC, INC (9)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make 2 single crochets in the first stitch
Make 1 single crochet in each of the next 5 stitches
Make 2 single crochets in the last stitch
👉 Stitch count: 9

HEAD (continues)
ROW 5-6: 9 SC (9)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make 1 single crochet in each stitch across.
👉 Stitch count: 9
ROW 7: DEC, 5 SC, DEC (7)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make a decrease* across the first 2 stitches
Make 1 single crochet in each of the next 5 stitches
Make a decrease across the last 2 stitches
👉 Stitch count: 7
*DECREASE
A decrease means turning two
stitches into one.
ROW 8: DEC, 3 SC, DEC (5)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make a decrease across the first 2 stitches
Make 1 single crochet in each of the next 3 stitches
Make a decrease across the last 2 stitches
👉 Stitch count: 5
HOW TO:
1. Insert your hook into the next
working stitch and pull up a
loop. (You now have 2 loops
on your hook)
2. Insert your hook into the next
stitch and pull up a loop. (You
now have 3 loops on your
hook)
3. Yarn over and pull through all
3 loops on the hook
✅ Two stitches are now combined
into one = decrease
ROW 9: DEC, 1 SC, DEC (3)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make a decrease across the first 2 stitches
Make 1 single crochet in the next stitch
Make a decrease across the last 2 stitches
Don't FO, keep going to BODY
👉 Stitch count: 3

BODY (continues)
ROW 14–19: 11 SC (11)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make 11 single crochet in all stitches across.
👉 Stitch count: 11
Place a stitch marker in the space between Row 14 and Row 15. (This is where you will begin

Repeat this row for a total of 5 rows.
Turn your work and chain 1 after each row.
ROW 20: DEC, 7 SC, DEC (9)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make a decrease across the first 2 stitches
Make 1 single crochet in each of the next 7 stitches
Make a decrease across the last 2 stitches
Don't FO, keep going to LEG 1
👉 Stitch count: 9
LEGS
At this point, you will begin forming the legs directly from the body.
👉 Continue working with the same yarn. Do not fasten off after Row 20.
You will now work only part of the row to create the first leg.
ROW 21 (Leg 1): 4 SC (4)
Chain 1.
Turn your work.
Make 1 single crochet in each of the first 4 stitches only.
👉 Stitch count: 4
Leave the remaining stitches unworked. These will be used later for the second leg.

Chain 1.
Turn your work.

Make 1 single crochet in each stitch across.

ARMS
Arms are added directly onto the body.
Locate the space between Row 11 and Row 12 on the side of the body.
Attach yarn at the desired position.
Chain 1.
Make 1 single crochet in the same space
Make 1 half double crochet in the next space
Make 3 double crochets in the next space
Chain 2
Make a slip stitch into the same space (where the 3 dc were made)
Fasten off.
Arm 2
Move to the opposite side of the body, approximately between Row 14 and Row 15.
Attach yarn.
Chain 2.
Make 3 double crochets in the same space
Make 1 half double crochet in the next space
Make 1 single crochet in the next space
Make a slip stitch to secure
Fasten off.
EARS
*SLIP STITCH
A slip stitch (ss)is a short
stitch used to join, move, or
finish without adding height.
HOW TO
1. Insert your hook into the
stitch
2. Yarn over
3. Pull the yarn straight
through the stitch and the
loop already on your hook
You will end with one loop on
your hook.
✅ This completes one slip
stitch
Ears are worked directly onto the top corners of the head.
Ear 1
Attach yarn in the top corner of the head.
Chain 2.
Make 2 double crochets in the next space
Chain 2
Make a slip stitch into the same space
Fasten off.
Ear 2
Move to the opposite top corner, at the same
level as the last stitch of the first ear.
Attach yarn.
Chain 2.
Make 2 double crochets in the same
space
Chain 2
Make a slip stitch to secure
Fasten off.

MAKE A SECOND PANEL
Repeat the entire pattern to create a second identical piece.
FINISHING & ASSEMBLY
Before joining the two pieces:
Weave in Ends
Using a yarn needle, weave in all loose ends securely on the wrong side of the work.
Add eyes and nose
Use safety eyes, or
Embroider using yarn (suggested position of the eyes - between row 4 and 5)
👉 This step must be done before assembling.
Joining the Pieces
Place both panels together with wrong sides facing each other.
Attach yarn at any point along the edge.
Work slip stitches evenly around, inserting your hook through both layers
Continue around the entire shape to join
👉 Optional: Lightly stuff the bear before fully closing, or leave flat for a keychain-style finish.
Fasten off and weave in all ends securely.
